CASE STUDY: V2

Wellington Living Room Transformation

A masterclass in intentionality. Turning a cluttered, disjointed family space into an editorial-grade sanctuary of calm and curated comfort.

The Challenge

The Wellington project began with a room that had "stopped breathing." Years of accumulation had obscured its architectural bones, leaving the client overwhelmed and unable to relax in their own home.

grid_view

Visual Noise

Conflicting patterns and excessive small-scale decor created a fragmented visual experience that felt restless.

shortcut

Stagnant Flow

Bulky furniture blocked natural traffic paths and obscured the garden views, making the 40sqm space feel cramped.

Julie's Approach

A three-stage restorative process designed to reveal the home's true potential through editing and intentionality.

01

Spatial Editing

We removed the 'unnecessary' to find the room's heartbeat. This involved auditing every piece of furniture for both utility and beauty.

  • check_circle Cleared sightlines to the garden
  • check_circle Reprioritized architectural focal points
02

Tonal Alignment

Introduction of a 'Curated Sanctuary' palette. Using Slate and Mint tones to ground the space while allowing natural light to bounce.

  • check_circle Monochromatic base for cohesion
  • check_circle Gold accents for warmth and quality
03

The Curation Layer

Strategic placement of 'hero' objects. We treated each surface as an editorial layout, balancing texture, height, and history.

  • check_circle Layered textiles for tactile comfort
  • check_circle Symmetrical and asymmetrical balance
